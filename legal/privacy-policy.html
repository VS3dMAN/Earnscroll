2. Camera and On-Device Machine Learning

EarnScroll uses your device's camera to detect and count exercises (squats, pushups, planks) in real time. Here is exactly how it works:

  1. When you start a workout, the camera captures live video frames.
  2. Each frame is resized to 192×192 pixels and processed entirely on your device by a TensorFlow Lite pose-estimation model (MoveNet Lightning).
  3. The model extracts body-joint positions (keypoints) from the frame. Joint angles are calculated to detect exercise form and count repetitions.
  4. The original camera frame is immediately discarded from memory after keypoints are extracted. Frames are never saved to storage, never recorded, and never transmitted off your device.
  5. Only the resulting exercise rep counts are saved to your device's local storage.
Key point: No photos, videos, or images ever leave your device. All pose-estimation processing happens locally using on-device machine learning. We have no ability to access, view, or retrieve your camera feed.

3. Accessibility Service and App Blocking

EarnScroll uses Android's AccessibilityService API to provide its app-blocking feature. When your earned screen-time minutes run out, the accessibility service detects when you open a blocked app and displays a full-screen overlay prompting you to earn more time through exercise.

What the accessibility service accesses:

  • Window state changes — which app is currently in the foreground, so the service can determine if a blocked app has been launched.
  • Package names — the identity of the foreground app, compared against your personal block list.

What the accessibility service does NOT access:

  • Screen content, text, passwords, or input fields
  • Notifications or messages
  • Clipboard data
  • Any data from other apps beyond the package name
Key point: All accessibility service data stays entirely on your device in encrypted storage. We never transmit app-usage data, your block list, or which apps you use to any server.

Prominent In-App Disclosure

Before the App requests you to enable the accessibility service, a dedicated disclosure screen will explain exactly what data is accessed, how it is used, and that it remains on your device. You must provide affirmative consent (e.g., tapping "I Understand") before the permission request is shown. You can revoke access at any time via your device settings (Settings → Accessibility → EarnScroll).

7. Data Storage and Security

7.1 On-Device Storage

The majority of your data — workout history, time bank, streak, app preferences, and blocking settings — is stored exclusively on your device. Sensitive data (such as subscription status and your block list) is stored in encrypted storage backed by your device's hardware security. Time-bank data is protected with HMAC-based integrity signing to prevent tampering.

7.2 Cloud Storage

Only your account credentials and session data are stored on Supabase's cloud servers, which use industry-standard encryption at rest and in transit (TLS 1.2+). Supabase hosts data on AWS infrastructure.

7.3 Security Measures

We employ the following security measures:

  • Hardware-backed encrypted storage (Expo SecureStore / Android EncryptedSharedPreferences) for sensitive local data
  • HMAC-SHA256 integrity signing for time-bank and workout data to prevent tampering
  • Device-unique cryptographic keys generated securely and stored in the device keychain
  • TLS encryption for all data transmitted to Supabase
  • No plain-text storage of passwords (handled entirely by Supabase's auth service)
